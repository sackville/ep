# Repository Working Model

## Purpose

This repository is the canonical body of knowledge for Enterprise Playability and its related work.

Its purpose is not merely to store documents, but to preserve shared understanding as that understanding evolves.

Every document in this repository represents the team's best current understanding of a subject. As new discoveries are made, the repository should evolve to reflect them.

---

## Shared Understanding

The repository exists to reduce reliance on individual memory.

Instead of asking contributors to remember past conversations, design meetings, or email threads, the repository preserves the understanding that emerged from those conversations.

This distinction is intentional.

The goal is not to preserve history.

The goal is to preserve understanding.

---

## Conversations

Conversations remain an essential part of the design process.

Their purpose is to:

- explore ideas,
- challenge assumptions,
- discover new concepts,
- reconcile competing viewpoints,
- and refine language.

Conversations are exploratory.

They are expected to contain mistakes, dead ends, and incomplete ideas.

Not every idea discussed deserves a permanent place in the repository.

---

## Promotion

When an idea becomes sufficiently stable that it should guide future work, it should be promoted into the repository.

Promotion is the deliberate act of converting conversation into durable shared understanding.

Once promoted, the repository—not the conversation—becomes the authoritative source.

---

## Working Principles

The repository follows several principles.

### Markdown First

All long-lived knowledge should be stored as Markdown whenever practical.

Markdown is durable, versionable, searchable, diffable, and easily transformed into other formats.

### Public by Default

Enterprise Playability is being developed in the open.

Unless legal, contractual, or ethical concerns require otherwise, both the ideas and the reasoning behind them should remain publicly visible.

### Transparent Decisions

Important decisions should be accompanied by their rationale.

YTHO and MADR decision records exist to preserve not only what was decided, but why.

### Continuous Refinement

No document is considered complete.

Every document represents the team's current best understanding and is expected to evolve.

---

## AI Collaboration

AI collaborators should treat this repository as the primary source of project context.

Conversations are opportunities to discover new understanding.

The repository is where that understanding is preserved.

When repository content conflicts with memory from a previous conversation, the repository takes precedence.

---

## Relationship to Enterprise Playability

This repository intentionally practices the principles that Enterprise Playability promotes.

It serves as the project's collective working memory, allowing both humans and AI collaborators to spend less effort reconstructing context and more effort extending shared understanding.
