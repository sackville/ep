# Framework Relationships: Overlaps, Seams, Tensions, and Unique Contributions

*Step 3 output for the EP process reconciliation. This document interprets the [framework crosswalk](framework-crosswalk.md) without yet defining the shared EP commitments or an integrated process.*

## Central finding

The six frameworks do not describe six competing versions of one process. They inspect the same intervention at different altitudes and against different kinds of failure.

They share a common empirical posture:

```text
Understand something real
  → make intent and uncertainty explicit
  → create a bounded intervention
  → expose it to reality
  → learn and change
```

But they do not mean the same thing by *real*, *intervention*, *evidence*, or *success*. Their value lies partly in the overlap and partly in maintaining those distinctions.

The reconciliation should therefore produce a choreography of questions and evidence, not a winner, a stack of mandatory ceremonies, or a claim that all six frameworks were saying the same thing all along.

## Terms used in this analysis

### Overlap

Two or more lenses express substantially the same underlying commitment, even when they apply it to different subjects.

### Seam

The output, question, or evidence from one lens becomes useful input to another. A seam is a productive handoff, not duplicated work.

### Tension

Two valid concerns pull decisions in different directions. A tension requires situated judgment and should not be erased by choosing one universal rule.

### Unique contribution

A question or protection that no other lens supplies with comparable precision. Other lenses may touch it, but cannot safely substitute for it.

## Genuine overlaps

### 1. Outcomes matter more than output

Every lens resists treating implementation as success:

- UCD asks whether the person made progress on the job.
- Lean/BMC asks whether a product or business belief gained credible evidence.
- Game design asks whether intended dynamics and experience emerged.
- DDD asks whether the model and behavior remain truthful to the domain.
- BDD asks whether the agreed significant behavior occurred.
- Agile asks whether a usable increment advances the product goal and generates feedback.

This is a genuine shared commitment. The lenses differ in which outcome they evaluate, so their evidence must remain distinct.

### 2. Real situations outrank abstract speculation

All six prefer concrete contact with reality:

- Actual people, work, artifacts, and context.
- Actual customer and buyer behavior.
- Actual play rather than design critique alone.
- Actual domain episodes and exceptions.
- Actual examples rather than vague requirements.
- Actual usable increments rather than component progress.

This common grounding is stronger than a generic injunction to “be user focused.” It identifies abstraction without return to reality as a cross-framework failure mode.

### 3. Uncertainty should be visible and actionable

Each lens treats uncertainty as legitimate work:

- Incomplete understanding of a persona or job.
- Unvalidated product and business hypotheses.
- Unproven links among mechanics, dynamics, and experience.
- Open modeling questions.
- Questions exposed by example mapping.
- Risk and learning reflected in backlog order.

The shared move is not to eliminate uncertainty up front, but to name it well enough to decide what evidence is needed next.

### 4. Shared language enables shared work

All lenses depend on participants being able to discuss the same thing coherently. UCD grounds language in human work; Lean/BMC distinguishes business actors and hypotheses; game design names roles, verbs, loops, and feedback; DDD establishes domain meaning; BDD tests meaning through examples; Agile carries it into goals, stories, and reviews.

The genuine overlap is commitment to shared understanding. The seam lies in how the vocabulary is discovered, tested, and used.

### 5. Concrete examples are a primary discovery tool

Real episodes serve all six:

- UCD reconstructs situated work.
- Lean/BMC turns assumptions into testable cases and thresholds.
- Game design observes specific moments of action and feedback.
- DDD discovers events, rules, and boundaries.
- BDD makes examples the center of collaborative discovery.
- Agile uses them to clarify thin increments and acceptance.

BDD gives this practice its most explicit form, but does not own the raw material or all uses of it.

### 6. Smallness is relational, not merely fewer features

Each lens defines a different minimum:

- Enough to help one persona perform one situated job.
- Enough to change confidence in one consequential hypothesis.
- Enough for one important dynamic or loop to emerge.
- Enough domain model to preserve coherence in one capability.
- Enough examples to agree on significant behavior.
- Enough end-to-end capability to be usable and reviewable.

The overlap is disciplined bounding. A small artifact that fails the relevant test is not meaningfully minimal.

### 7. Feedback must arrive while change remains affordable

UCD evaluation, customer experiments, playtesting, domain-model challenge, executable examples, and incremental review all shorten different feedback loops. All reject discovering foundational mistakes only after large commitment.

The feedback sources are not interchangeable, but the timing principle is shared.

### 8. Artifacts are living representations, not frozen truth

Personas, canvases, pillars, domain models, examples, and backlogs are expected to evolve with evidence. Each is a current representation of understanding, useful only while it remains connected to reality.

This does not mean every artifact should change casually. It means stability must come from accumulated confidence and continued explanatory value, not from document authority alone.

### 9. Expansion should follow evidence

Every lens contains a restraint against premature breadth:

- Mind uncovered jobs rather than averaging them into the current persona.
- Scale after demand and economics are evidenced.
- Add mechanics after the core experience works.
- add contexts or platform capabilities when domain demand appears.
- grow specifications with significant behavior.
- deliver additional scope incrementally.

This is a genuine overlap with direct affinity to the Viper demand-pull principle.

### 10. Learning must change something

All six reject feedback as a ceremonial endpoint. Evidence should update some combination of the persona, jobs, hypothesis confidence, canvas, mechanics, model, examples, goals, or backlog.

If observation does not have permission to alter the work, the loop is performative rather than empirical.

## Productive seams

### 1. Purpose to human benefit to near-term direction

```text
Durable organizational purpose
  → strategically important person and job
  → product and value hypothesis
  → intended interactive experience
  → domain vision
  → product or release goal
  → capability outcome
```

No lens supplies this entire chain. UCD gives purpose a human referent; Lean/BMC makes value and viability hypothetical; game design expresses the intended experience; DDD identifies the domain meaning; Agile provides near-term direction; BDD keeps capability behavior tied to an actor outcome.

The seam is healthy when traceability is visible without requiring a separate artifact at every arrow.

### 2. Observation to domain discovery to behavioral agreement

```text
Observed work and artifacts
  → real domain episodes and vocabulary
  → events, rules, and open questions
  → concrete examples and agreed outcomes
```

UCD and Lean/BMC bring contact with people and behavior. DDD interprets episodes as domain meaning. BDD challenges rules through examples. Game design adds attention to feedback and emergent behavior. The same episode may serve several lenses, but each extracts different evidence from it.

### 3. Choosing one meaningful slice

The lenses can jointly constrain a slice without voting on features:

```text
Lean/BMC: Which uncertainty is most consequential and least evidenced?
UCD: For which person, job, and situation?
Game design: Which verb, loop, or experiential hypothesis must become playable?
DDD: Which coherent domain capability carries the behavior?
BDD: Which examples establish what it must do?
Agile: What is the smallest usable end-to-end increment?
```

This is one of the strongest seams in the analysis. Each question removes a different kind of false smallness.

### 4. Domain truth to playable representation

DDD distinguishes reality from representation and supplies domain semantics. UCD determines what the person needs to understand and do. Game design shapes attention, action, feedback, and progression. BDD separates domain outcomes from representation outcomes and makes significant behavior observable.

The handoff is not “the model dictates the UI.” It is a reciprocal design seam:

- The representation must not falsify the domain.
- The domain model should not dictate an unusable interface.
- Difficulty representing something may reveal a missing or confused domain concept.
- A delightful representation does not justify inventing certainty.

### 5. Desired experience to testable behavior

UCD and game design can state qualities such as clarity, confidence, agency, curiosity, and relief. BDD can make supporting behavior concrete, while warning against reducing adaptive human outcomes to checklists. Agile carries the verifiable portion into Done. UCD and playtesting retain judgment over what cannot be fully specified.

This seam preserves both rigor and humanity: specify what can be agreed without pretending that passing criteria proves delight or progress.

### 6. Prototype to tracer bullet

UCD may need an attemptable interaction, Lean/BMC a credible experiment, and game design a playable loop before production architecture exists. DDD and BDD clarify the behavior that must survive into a real system. Agile turns sufficient learning into a thin operable implementation.

Prototype and tracer bullet are related but not synonyms:

- A prototype may be disposable and optimized for learning.
- A tracer bullet is an end-to-end production path intended to remain and grow.

The seam is deciding when uncertainty has fallen enough to invest in the durable path.

### 7. Multiple evaluations of one intervention

One working slice can support several evaluations:

```text
Does it preserve domain truth?                 DDD
Does it produce agreed observable behavior?   BDD
Can the persona accomplish the job?            UCD
Do intended dynamics and experience emerge?   Game design
Does behavior support the value hypothesis?    Lean/BMC
Can the team deliver, operate, and adapt it?    Agile
```

This is not six rounds of approval. It is one intervention producing several kinds of evidence, collected at appropriate times and fidelities.

### 8. Learning back into shared memory

The feedback loops terminate in different artifacts, but their updates should remain mutually intelligible:

- Human understanding updates persona and job artifacts.
- Market evidence updates hypotheses and canvases.
- Play updates mechanics, pillars, and loops.
- Domain discovery updates language and models.
- Behavioral agreement updates examples and specifications.
- Delivery learning updates goals, backlog, and quality practice.

The seam requires traceability of *why* an artifact changed without creating a central document that duplicates them all.

### 9. Demand-pulled expansion

Lean/BMC supplies evidence of demand and economics; UCD identifies additional people and jobs; game design reveals needs for deeper mechanics or content; DDD reveals new contexts and platform capabilities; BDD grows behavioral coverage; Agile delivers expansion incrementally.

Viper can act as the sequencing discipline across this seam: capability grows behind demonstrated application need.

## Semantic collisions: similar words that must not be collapsed

| Word or phrase | Distinct meanings to preserve |
| --- | --- |
| User, customer, player, actor, persona | The user operates the product; customer may buy it; player experiences its interactive system; domain actor participates in domain behavior; persona is the selected design focus carrying situated jobs. One person may occupy several roles, but the roles are not synonymous. |
| Value | Human progress, desired experience, business benefit, domain outcome, and delivered increment are related but independently falsifiable. |
| Outcome | Job progress, experiential result, business evidence, domain state change, agreed behavior, and product-goal advancement require different observation. |
| Model | Persona model, Business Model Canvas, game-system model, domain model, example model, and delivery plan represent different realities and should not be merged into one master model. |
| Hypothesis | Persona understanding, value proposition, mechanics-to-dynamics link, domain interpretation, expected behavior, and delivery forecast carry different evidence standards. |
| Test | Usability study, market experiment, playtest, model challenge, executable scenario, and software test answer different questions. Passing one says little about the others unless the inference is explicit. |
| Prototype | A UCD prototype makes a task attemptable; a Lean experiment tests a belief; a game prototype produces dynamics. Any may be disposable. |
| MVP | A product capable of producing validated learning, often interpreted as a first marketable product; it is not automatically the same as a thin story, prototype, or core-loop test. |
| Tracer bullet or walking skeleton | A durable end-to-end implementation path that proves integration and can grow; it need not yet prove market value or experience quality. |
| Scenario | A situated UCD narrative, game beat, domain episode, BDD example, or agile acceptance scenario may describe the same event at different resolution. |
| Done | Passing a Definition of Done establishes agreed delivery quality. It does not by itself establish human progress, delightful experience, domain completeness, or business viability. |
| Feedback | User observation, player behavior, domain-expert correction, test result, delivery review, and market commitment are different evidence streams. |

## Irreducible tensions

### 1. One-person design focus versus market breadth

**Valid concern from UCD:** A feature designed for several averaged personas loses situated coherence.

**Valid concern from Lean/BMC:** A viable business needs evidence about segments, buyers, channels, and repeatability beyond one favorite person.

**Risk if collapsed:** Treating a market segment as a persona produces generic design; treating one delighted person as the market produces false commercial confidence.

**Question to preserve:** How do we retain one persona per feature while testing whether strategically similar jobs and buying conditions recur across a market?

### 2. Human value versus commercial viability

**Valid concern from UCD and game design:** The product must help and respect the person experiencing it.

**Valid concern from Lean/BMC:** Someone must adopt, authorize, fund, and sustain delivery at viable cost.

**Risk if collapsed:** Willingness to pay is mistaken for human benefit, or human benefit is assumed to create a business automatically.

**Question to preserve:** What evidence establishes desirability, viability, and responsible experience separately before a decision combines them?

### 3. Deep understanding versus rapid learning

**Valid concern from DDD, UCD, and BDD:** Important language, jobs, rules, and examples must be understood well enough to avoid building falsehoods.

**Valid concern from Lean and Agile:** Large up-front discovery can delay the real-world feedback that would invalidate it.

**Risk if collapsed:** Either analysis becomes an indefinite prerequisite, or speed becomes permission to encode a confused model.

**Question to preserve:** What is the minimum understanding required to make the next experiment credible and safe, and which questions can remain open?

### 4. Domain truth versus cognitive simplification

**Valid concern from DDD:** The system must preserve meaningful distinctions and remain truthful as reality changes.

**Valid concern from UCD and game design:** The representation must reduce cognitive cost, guide attention, and avoid overwhelming the person.

**Risk if collapsed:** The UI exposes raw domain complexity, or simplification conceals uncertainty and important consequence.

**Question to preserve:** Which truths must be visible now, which can be progressively disclosed, and which must never be hidden?

### 5. Specified behavior versus emergent experience

**Valid concern from BDD and DDD:** Significant rules and outcomes should be agreed, coherent, and verifiable.

**Valid concern from game design and UCD:** Interacting mechanics and human interpretation produce dynamics that cannot be fully specified in advance.

**Risk if collapsed:** Passing scenarios is treated as proof of a good experience, or emergence is used to excuse incorrect behavior.

**Question to preserve:** Which behavior must conform exactly, and which experience must be discovered through observation and tuning?

### 6. Thin slices versus a coherent loop

**Valid concern from Agile and Lean:** Smaller increments reduce batch risk and accelerate evidence.

**Valid concern from game design and UCD:** A fragment that omits the action–feedback loop or situated job may be impossible to evaluate meaningfully.

**Valid concern from DDD:** A slice must preserve coherent domain behavior and history.

**Risk if collapsed:** The smallest story produces no real experience, while insisting on the whole loop creates a large first release.

**Question to preserve:** What is the thinnest end-to-end slice that retains the job, loop, domain coherence, and evidence needed?

### 7. Cheap prototypes versus representative fidelity

**Valid concern from Lean and Agile:** Invest as little as necessary before important assumptions are tested.

**Valid concern from UCD and game design:** A prototype without representative content, feedback, or visual character may test the wrong experience.

**Risk if collapsed:** Polished waste on one side; invalid feedback from an unrepresentative artifact on the other.

**Question to preserve:** Which dimensions of fidelity are causal to the hypothesis, and which can remain crude?

### 8. Stable language versus evolving understanding

**Valid concern from DDD, BDD, and Agile:** Shared terms and examples must remain stable enough for coordinated implementation.

**Valid concern from UCD, Lean, and game design:** Research, experiments, and play frequently reveal that the current framing is wrong.

**Risk if collapsed:** Vocabulary churn destroys coordination, or terminological stability fossilizes a false model.

**Question to preserve:** What confidence or evidence warrants changing established language, and how is the change propagated?

### 9. Encodable acceptance versus adaptive human judgment

**Valid concern from BDD and Agile:** Observable criteria and automation provide clarity, regression protection, and a meaningful Done.

**Valid concern from UCD and game design:** Whether a person made progress or experienced clarity cannot be exhausted by a checklist.

**Risk if collapsed:** Subjective claims escape verification, or proxy metrics replace the human outcome.

**Question to preserve:** What should be automated, what should be observed, and where must informed judgment remain explicit?

### 10. Dogfooding access versus external validity

**Valid concern from UCD and Agile:** Building for a known real person offers exceptionally short feedback loops.

**Valid concern from Lean/BMC:** The builder-user relationship can create confirmation bias and says little by itself about external demand or adoption.

**Risk if collapsed:** External research delays a valuable internal tool, or internal enthusiasm is generalized into a market.

**Question to preserve:** Which claims can dogfooding support, and which require evidence from other users, buyers, or contexts?

### 11. Durable memory versus maintenance burden

**Valid concern from DDD, BDD, and Command's purpose:** Decisions, behavior, rationale, and changed understanding should remain explainable.

**Valid concern from Lean, Agile, and UCD:** Documentation and data maintenance can exceed the value produced and reduce adoption.

**Risk if collapsed:** The system forgets why it is true, or becomes too burdensome to keep truthful.

**Question to preserve:** What is the lightest durable record that preserves the future understanding the job actually requires?

### 12. Fast delivery feedback versus slow business evidence

**Valid concern from Agile, BDD, and automated delivery:** Behavior and integration can be checked continuously.

**Valid concern from Lean/BMC:** Adoption, purchasing, retention, partnership, and unit economics may require much longer cycles.

**Risk if collapsed:** Shipping cadence is mistaken for business learning, or slow market cycles stall all product learning.

**Question to preserve:** Which nested feedback loops can continue while slower evidence accumulates, and which commitments should wait?

### 13. Individual focus versus shared organizational understanding

**Valid concern from persona-driven UCD:** One persona per feature preserves design coherence.

**Valid concern from Command's mission and domain:** Shared understanding exists among several people with different perspectives and responsibilities.

**Risk if collapsed:** A committee-designed interface delights no one, or a personally effective tool fails to establish shared understanding.

**Question to preserve:** Whose job governs a feature, how do others participate or benefit, and when does a distinct job warrant another persona or feature?

### 14. Product integrity versus local optimization

**Valid concern from each specialized lens:** Its failure mode can be consequential enough to demand correction.

**Valid concern from the whole:** Maximizing every criterion simultaneously can create ceremony, delay, and a product designed by checklist.

**Risk if collapsed:** One lens dominates, or every lens becomes a mandatory approval gate.

**Question to preserve:** Which lens is decision-relevant for the present uncertainty, and what minimum cross-check protects the whole?

## Unique contributions that must survive reconciliation

### User-Centered Design: the situated human test

UCD uniquely preserves a real person's job, context, motives, constraints, and progress as the unit of design judgment. No other lens prevents aggregation into a fictional average with equal force.

If lost, EP could become technically, behaviorally, and commercially coherent while failing the person at the desk.

### Lean Startup and BMC: viability under uncertainty

Lean/BMC uniquely separates durable purpose from falsifiable product and business beliefs, then demands evidence about buyers, channels, revenue, costs, partnerships, and repeatability.

If lost, EP could create valuable and delightful products without discovering whether a sustainable business can bring them to people.

### Game design: emergent interactive experience

Game design uniquely examines repeated verbs, feedback loops, progression, game feel, and the dynamics that emerge when mechanics interact with human interpretation.

If lost, EP could use playful aesthetics or gamification components without actually designing playability.

### Domain-Driven Design: semantic and behavioral truth

DDD uniquely provides disciplined language, context boundaries, invariants, and domain models that remain distinct from UI and persistence.

If lost, EP could build a compelling representation that becomes brittle or untruthful as domain complexity appears.

### Behavior-Driven Development: agreement through examples

BDD uniquely turns conversation into a disciplined discovery practice centered on rules, examples, and questions, with a path to executable shared specifications.

If lost, EP could carry elegant concepts into implementation while participants unknowingly mean different things.

### Agile development: the work system for continuous delivery and adaptation

Agile uniquely addresses how a team repeatedly turns priorities into small usable, operable increments and changes its plan as feedback arrives.

If lost, EP could contain excellent discovery and design guidance without a reliable means of converting it into working software.

## What should not be combined into one artifact

Some artifacts may link or share source evidence, but combining them would erase meaningful distinctions:

- Persona and customer segment.
- Job statement and value proposition.
- Business Model Canvas and domain model.
- Current-state journey and event storm.
- Core gameplay loop and business acquisition loop.
- Domain invariant and design pillar.
- BDD scenario and usability task.
- Executable specification and playtest plan.
- Hypothesis register and product backlog.
- Definition of Done and success criteria for the product or business.
- Prototype and tracer bullet.

The goal is coherent traceability among these artifacts, not a universal template containing all of them.

## Candidate implications for the next steps

These are inputs to step 4, not yet accepted EP commitments:

1. The reconciled framework likely needs a common empirical spine with distinct evaluation questions.
2. Evidence types should be named and preserved separately rather than rolled into one confidence score.
3. One intervention should be designed to answer several compatible questions where possible, without pretending one observation answers them all.
4. “Minimum” should mean minimally credible for the selected person, hypothesis, experience, domain behavior, and delivery path—not universally smallest.
5. The framework should pull lenses and artifacts according to the uncertainty at hand rather than require every ceremony in every cycle.
6. Human progress, domain truth, agreed behavior, experiential quality, business viability, and delivery quality should remain distinguishable definitions of success.
7. Shared memory should preserve consequential learning and decisions while remaining light enough to maintain.
8. Expansion should follow evidence, but evidence requirements differ by the claim being made.

## Open questions carried forward

- What shared commitments can govern all work without becoming slogans?
- What minimum cross-checks are always required, even when one lens leads?
- How should the framework choose which uncertainty is most important now?
- Can one concise framing artifact link purpose, persona/job, hypothesis, experience, domain capability, and next increment without duplicating their authoritative artifacts?
- Which evaluation questions belong in every cycle, and which are conditional?
- How should slow business-learning loops coexist with fast design, behavior, and delivery loops?
- What language should EP use for the integrated intervention: experiment, slice, probe, increment, or something else?
- How should decisions and evidence be preserved without creating reconciliation bureaucracy?
