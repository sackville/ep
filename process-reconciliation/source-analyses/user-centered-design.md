# Viper Command Through the User-Centered Design Lens

*Preserved source analysis for EP process reconciliation. This is a situated assessment grounded in the EP persona guide, not reconciled EP guidance.*

## Governing concern

EP's persona-driven UCD treats the job as the atomic unit of analysis and the persona as the unit of design focus. A specific person carries a coherent bundle of real jobs in a real situation. The governing test is: **Does it get the job done for the persona?**

Viper Command is unusually far along in establishing that target, but has not yet closed the loop through observation, interactive testing, and iterative evaluation.

## Work completed or substantially underway

### Real-person-anchored primary persona

Scott is not a role abstraction or averaged founder. The persona records his responsibilities, collaborators, tools, habits, motivations, anxieties, context, and relationship to the product as user, builder, and collaborator.

### Coherent persona choice

Scott's real job list is dense with strategically important Command work: maintaining the integrated picture, facilitating formation, coordinating work and counsel, reconstructing context, building the app, and evaluating Viper. Formal population clustering was not necessary to invent for this dogfood starting point.

### Root and supporting jobs

The root job is to maintain sufficient shared understanding for the team to see the path to objectives and confidently decide what to do next. Supporting jobs include maintaining a durable picture, visualizing paths, understanding consequences, answering what's next, keeping the picture current, and defining or selecting unknown process.

The persona currently says “five MVP jobs” but numbers six; the introductory count is stale.

### Object and meta jobs

The object job is guiding the founders through formation and strategic decisions. The meta job is demonstrating that a playable application can facilitate decisions and preserve shared understanding. This prevents the experiment from replacing the user's real need.

### Failure modes and context of use

The project records partial context, scattered memory, dependency and task blindness, diverging mental models, and repeated conversations. It also describes intermittent work, counsel, meetings, screen sharing, information sources, and Scott's role as manual synchronizer.

### Situated scenarios

The six beats connect jobs to meaningful situations: returning after time away, advancing work, preparing for counsel, absorbing new information, and encountering undefined process.

### Persona-derived design

The warm tech tree responds to Scott's enjoyment of systems, dependencies, consequences, and the question “What's next?” The design principles trace directly to his jobs.

### Representations and human success criteria

Visual frames make proposed states discussable. The persona defines both questions Scott should be able to answer and how he should feel: relieved, confident, curious, inspired, and ready to help.

## Recommended next work

### 1. Validate the persona against recent behavior

Reconstruct actual episodes such as counsel-call preparation, returning after time away, discovering unowned work, revisiting a settled question, absorbing a legal change, and choosing the next action. Inspect emails, notes, whiteboards, records, and task lists rather than relying only on memory.

For each episode capture trigger, objective, information needed, sources consulted, missing context, workaround, meaning of progress, and points of divergence.

### 2. Refine and prioritize the job structure

Confirm the relationships among the root job, object and meta jobs, six MVP jobs, scenarios, and deferred jobs. Select one primary target for the first usable experience, such as restoring enough context after time away to choose the next valuable action.

Keep jobs not selected on a roadmap to mind the gaps; do not blend them into the first feature for coverage.

### 3. Map the current-state experience

Create a lightweight journey from the trigger to confident next action. Capture actions, artifacts, people, questions, breakdowns, emotions, workarounds, and outcomes. Use it as a baseline and a guard against merely digitizing the current process.

### 4. Define one persona, job, and scenario per initial feature

For example, design orientation for Scott returning after several days whose job is to restore enough context to identify what deserves attention. Design the cascade for Scott after progress whose job is to understand what changed and became possible.

### 5. Clarify secondary personas

Russ and Cynthia may benefit without becoming joint primary personas. Beat 3 should say whether it primarily serves Scott's facilitation job, Russ's understanding job, Cynthia's relevant-work job, or distinct features for each. Add a second persona only for a genuinely different job.

### 6. Make the scenes attemptable

Create low-cost interactive or facilitated flows for finding actionable work, explaining locks, identifying ownership, completing a Task, settling a Decision, seeing consequences, preparing for counsel, absorbing change, and handling undefined process.

### 7. Evaluate with representative content and tasks

Use real or carefully redacted formation data. Observe attention, interpretation of regions and hue, differentiation of kind and state, understanding of locks and next actions, trust in history, expected interaction, and ignored or misunderstood information. Give minimal coaching.

### 8. Define job-centered evaluation

Possible measures include time to identify work, correctness of the next action, explanation of blockers and ownership, understanding after progress, ability to resume without another source, number of external artifacts needed, confidence explaining the board, and effort to keep it truthful.

### 9. Evaluate accessibility and perceptual robustness

Test color vision, contrast, screen-sharing compression, displays, zoom and text scaling, keyboard use, reduced motion, understanding without animation, and reliance on atmospheric effects. Hue can map ownership without being the only means of identifying it.

### 10. Iterate persona and design separately

Update the persona when understanding of the person, situation, or job changes. Update the design when a correctly understood job is not served. Do not rewrite the persona to justify a favored interaction.

### 11. Continue evaluation in working software

Observe voluntary opening, currentness, meeting use, trust after time away, continued context reconstruction elsewhere, discrepancy correction, founder reconvergence, and jobs that pull new capabilities into the app.

## Lens summary

Command already knows who it serves, in what situation, carrying which jobs, and why those jobs matter. The next UCD transition is to validate the persona against real episodes, prioritize one situated job, make the interaction attemptable, observe Scott using it with real work, and revise until it genuinely gets the job done.
